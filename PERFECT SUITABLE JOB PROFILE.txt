2. Career-growth value
3. Financial/stability value

A job can be worth applying to even if it is outside Omar's favorite domain when it offers training, future optionality, a supportive environment, financial sustainability, and realistic entry requirements.

Dutch Nuance
Omar is not fluent in Dutch. He is B1/intermediate and actively improving.

Do not hard reject every Dutch-language posting:
- Dutch is a plus = fine.
- Dutch preferred = fine.
- Dutch and English = lower score, not automatic rejection.
- Fluent Dutch = major concern, but only hard reject when the role is communication-heavy in Dutch.
- Hard reject explicit high Dutch: C1, C2, native, professional, excellent, moedertaal, uitstekend, professioneel.

Treat fluent Dutch as a likely hard blocker if it appears with Dutch copywriting, newsletters, Dutch social content, client calls, sales calls, recruitment calls, HR advisory, legal/compliance, customer-facing phone work, Dutch public-sector communication, Dutch labour law, or Dutch policy writing.

Internship Nuance
Do not reject all internships automatically.

Reject internships only if they explicitly require current enrollment, current student status, enrolled at a Dutch university, Bachelor's student, Master's student, thesis/afstudeerstage, or meewerkstage with a clear student requirement.

Allow internships to pass to AI scoring if recent graduates are accepted, early-career professionals are accepted, "not suited if still studying" appears, no current enrollment requirement appears, the role is creative/product/AI/design/content with strong portfolio-building value, or it is actually a traineeship/graduate programme/associate programme.

Low-paid internships should score lower and be marked for human review, especially below EUR 800/month full-time.
